# Visualization Potpourri, March 2018

Time to wake up from the eagereyes winter hibernation with an aromatic potpourri! This time, we have news about pies, stippling, colors, sorting algorithms, and a few more. Also a video of my collaborator Noeska singing the praises of medical visualization.

Danielle Albers Szafir was named one of [the *30 under 30* in Science by Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/profile/danielle-szafir/). You might remember her from [my VIS report last year](/blog/2017/ieee-vis-2017-best-papers-keynote-capstone) and [the portrait](/blog/2017/danielle-albers-szafir) I posted as the first (and so far only one…) in my portrait series (more portraits coming, I promise).

Pie news! Lisa Charlotte Rost has written a [nice piece about pie charts the on data wrapper blog](http://academy.datawrapper.de/article/127-what-to-consider-when-creating-a-pie-chart). Jon Schwabish is also starting to love pie charts, [using some colorful language](http://policyviz.com/2018/01/11/fk-it-lets-use-pie-charts/). Combining two of my interests, this [news piece using an ISOTYPE pie](https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/wonk/wp/2017/12/06/the-richest-1-percent-now-owns-more-of-the-countrys-wealth-than-at-any-time-in-the-past-50-years/?utm_term=.58531654de98) is pretty interesting.

Since somebody has been sleeping at the wheel on this site, it's worthwhile to link to the tireless [Andy Kirk's most notable developments in visualization, July through December 2017](http://www.visualisingdata.com/2018/01/10-significant-visualisation-developments-july-december-2017/). Also check out [January through June](http://www.visualisingdata.com/2017/07/10-significant-visualisation-developments-january-june-2017/) if you missed it.

Domingo Martín et al. recently released [Stippleshop](https://tobias.isenberg.cc/VideosAndDemos/Martin2017SDS) in a collaboration between Team AVIZ–Inria-Saclay, Paris, and the Computer Graphics Group at the University of Granada, Spain. Stippleshop serves as a demo for [their *Survey of Digital Stippling* paper](https://tobias.isenberg.cc/personal/papers/Martin_2017_SDS.pdf) and allows people to play with stippling effects on their own or provided images. In addition to the coolness of offering a small toolkit with a survey paper, I am happy to see the *shop naming tradition continue (PhotoShop, VolumeShop, RegistrationShop,...).

Morolin has made some some lovely short videos [visualizing the behavior of sorting algorithms](https://imgur.com/gallery/GD5gi). They started out doing this [in a rainbow colormap](https://imgur.com/a/voutF) that was burning my eyes, but redid it [in Viridis](https://bids.github.io/colormap/) later, and the people rejoiced.

Speaking of colors, [Elijah Meeks](http://elijahmeeks.com/) has written up some great advice for people on [picking good colors for data visualization in D3](https://medium.com/@Elijah_Meeks/color-advice-for-data-visualization-with-d3-js-33b5adc41c90). The recent release of D3 v5 killed the (often wrongly used) *category20* colorscale, and Elija discusses good options for what to use instead.
