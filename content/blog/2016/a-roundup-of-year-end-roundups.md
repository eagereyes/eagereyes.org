# A Roundup of Year-End News Graphics Roundups

The end of the year is always a good time to look back at the great work done in the world of news graphics – and this year in particular, to relive all the heartbreak and disillusionment. Here is a list of year-end news graphics round-ups for your enjoyment and edification.

- The New York Times, [*2016: The Year in Visual Stories and Graphics*](http://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2016/12/28/us/year-in-interactive-graphics.html?_r=0)
- The Washington Post, [ *16 in ’16: The year in must-see visualizations from The Post *](https://www.washingtonpost.com/graphics/national/2016-in-graphics/)
- Bloomberg, [*A Tumultuous Year Explained in 129 Graphics*](https://www.bloomberg.com/graphics/2016-in-graphics/)
- The Wall Street Journal, *[The Best WSJ Graphics and Visual Stories From 2016](http://www.wsj.com/graphics/graphics-year-in-review-2016/) *and separately, *[The Year in Markets: 2016 in Nine Charts](http://www.wsj.com/graphics/year-in-markets-2016-in-nine-charts) *(both behind paywall)
- FiveThirtyEight, [*The Best And Worst Data Stories Of 2016*](http://fivethirtyeight.com/features/the-best-and-worst-data-stories-of-2016/) (not just graphics, more commentary – unusual and worth it)
- Maarten Lambrecht has compiled his [*Charts of 2016*](http://www.maartenlambrechts.com/2016/12/21/the-charts-of-2016.html), which include some that aren’t on the above lists
- Andy Kirk’s summaries are always worthwhile as well, here are his [*Significant Visualization Developments for 2016, Part 1*](http://www.visualisingdata.com/2016/07/10-significant-visualisation-developments-january-june-2016/) and [*Part 2*](http://www.visualisingdata.com/2016/12/10-significant-visualisation-developments-july-december-2016/). He has also compiled [all of his significant developments postings back to 2011 in one convenient list](http://www.visualisingdata.com/2016/12/collection-significant-development-posts/).
- Lisa Charlotte Rost has compiled [a personal list of what she wrote about](https://lisacharlotterost.github.io/2016/12/18/year-in-review/). If you’re not following her writing yet, this is a good starting point.
