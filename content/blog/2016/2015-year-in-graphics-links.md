A bit late, but here are links to a few round-ups of graphical journalism work from last year. These are always worth a look, no matter what time of year.

- *Bloomberg, *[2015 in Graphics](http://www.bloomberg.com/graphics/2015-in-graphics/)
- *The New York Times*, [2015: The Year in Visual Stories and Graphics](http://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2015/us/year-in-interactive-storytelling.html)
- *ProPublica, *[The 2015 ProPublica Year in Visual Storytelling](https://www.propublica.org/article/2015-propublica-visuals-and-interactives-year-in-review)
- *The Wall Street Journal*, [The Year in Interactive Graphics](http://graphics.wsj.com/wsj-interactives-2015/)
- *The Washington Post*, [2015: A Year in Graphics](https://www.washingtonpost.com/graphics/national/2015-in-graphics/)

And as a little palate cleanser, don't miss Quartz's *[most misleading charts of 2015, fixed](http://qz.com/580859/the-most-misleading-charts-of-2015-fixed/)* and Fivethirtyeight's *[47 Weirdest Charts From 2015](http://fivethirtyeight.com/features/our-47-weirdest-charts-from-2015/)*.
